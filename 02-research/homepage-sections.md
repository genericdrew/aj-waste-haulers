## 5. Recommended Section Sequence

### Evaluation Framework

**UX Laws Applied:**
- **Serial Position Effect (Primacy + Recency):** Users best remember what comes first and last. The hero (first) must lock in the core thesis. The CTA banner (second-to-last) and footer (last) must close the loop with a conversion action and trust confirmation respectively.
- **Progressive Disclosure:** Reveal information in order of decision-making need. Visitor arrives with: "Can you help me? Should I trust you? What do you offer? Are you near me? What do others say? What happens next?" The section order must mirror this cognitive sequence.
- **Law of Common Region / Gestalt Grouping:** Trust signals must cluster early and reappear later — trust strip below hero, testimonials mid-page, credentials in footer. Repetition across three positions is intentional, not redundant.
- **Hick's Law:** Fewer choices at each decision point reduces cognitive load. The audience pathway section (position 3) must offer exactly 3 clear paths — not more.
- **Peak-End Rule:** Visitors remember the peak moment and the end. The Why AJ / proof-of-pumpage section is the designed peak. The CTA banner is the designed end-action.

**Thesis Alignment:**
Per strategic-validation.md §5: "AJ is the only liquid waste company in Central Texas that proves every service call with documented measurement — in a market where every competitor fails to show homeowners the evidence they need to trust a stranger with their critical infrastructure."

The sequence must: (1) establish the thesis immediately (hero); (2) prove it credentially (trust strip); (3) differentiate it mechanically (Why AJ / proof section); (4) and convert it commercially (CTA banner). Everything else fills in the substance between those four structural pillars.

---

### Final Section Sequence

**1. Hero**
- **Rationale (Primacy):** First impression locks thesis. Per strategic-validation.md §7: "The headline should name the proof-of-pumpage distinction explicitly rather than defaulting to the vertical's generic pattern." Hero headline must name the gallon-meter differentiator or the "you pay only for what we remove" promise — not generic "Reliable Septic Service."
- **Content:** Headline (5–10 words, proof-of-pumpage framed), subheadline (1–2 sentences expanding the thesis + Hill Country rooting), Primary CTA: "Get a Free Quote" (links to multi-step form), Secondary CTA: phone number with click-to-call, Full-bleed background photo (branded truck + Hill Country landscape — AJ photography, no stock).
- **Gallon meter visible:** Show the meter or reference it in the eyebrow line ("Measured. Documented. Proven."). This is the strategic-validation.md mandate — the moat goes in the hero.
- **Trust signal placement:** Trust strip appears immediately below — do not stack trust signals inside hero itself, as they dilute the headline impact.

---

**2. Trust Strip**
- **Rationale (Serial Position + first credibility hit):** design-research.md §4 and strategic-validation.md §7 both mandate this position. Visitors deciding whether to call need licensed/insured/real reviews before they scroll. "Trust strip below hero = the first credibility hit" (design-research.md §4). The entire local competitive field has zero trust strips — this is white space to occupy.
- **Content:** 5 items in horizontal row on desktop, 2-column stack on mobile:
  1. TCEQ Licensed · [License Number] *(pending confirmation of AJ's TCEQ number — see strategic-validation.md G-04)*
  2. Licensed & Insured *(source: client-intake.md)*
  3. 40+ Years Combined Experience *(source: client-intake.md §Ownership — Ashman + Jones)*
  4. ⭐ [N]-Star Google Rating · [N] Reviews *(source: live Google Business Profile — see strategic-validation.md G-02)*
  5. 24/7 Emergency Service *(source: client-intake.md §Hours)*
- **Design:** Dark solid strip (navy or charcoal) with icon + short text per item. Contrasts with hero for visual break.
- **Trust categories covered:** Risk Reduction (TCEQ, insured), Competence (40+ years), Social Proof (Google rating), Risk Reduction (24/7). Spans 3 of 4 trust categories in a single strip — satisfies G02/G17 exit gate requirements.

---

**3. Audience Pathways**
- **Rationale (Hick's Law + Progressive Disclosure):** Three distinct audiences — residential homeowners, commercial/restaurant operators, and beverage-industry clients — have fundamentally different fears, services needed, and CTAs. Forcing them into a single undifferentiated flow loses each segment. Per strategic-validation.md §7: "Three different buyers with different fears and different CTAs." Three paths = exactly right per Hick's Law. No competitor provides audience-differentiated homepage entry points.
- **Content:** 3 cards, each with: audience label + icon, 1-sentence description of their primary service need, CTA link.
  - Card 1: "Homeowners" → septic pumping, emergency service, real estate inspections
  - Card 2: "Restaurants & Commercial" → grease trap compliance service, scheduled maintenance
  - Card 3: "Wineries, Breweries & Distilleries" → specialized liquid waste hauling (zero-competition niche)
- **Design:** 3-column on desktop, stacked on mobile. Each card styled distinctly but within the same color system.

---

**4. Services Grid**
- **Rationale (Progressive Disclosure — "What do you offer?"):** After audience pathways direct visitors to their segment, the services grid provides the full picture. 4/5 competitors use service cards with links to dedicated service pages. This section must link to individual service detail pages for SEO depth (trust-analysis.md §MUST — service detail pages 4/5 adoption).
- **Content:** 5–6 service cards:
  1. Septic Tank Pumping (residential)
  2. Grease Trap Cleaning (commercial)
  3. Beverage Industry Liquid Waste
  4. Emergency Pumping (24/7)
  5. Real Estate Septic Inspection
  6. Hard-to-Reach Tank Access (specialty — if content supports)
- **Each card:** Service icon (line-style, branded), service name, 1–2 sentence description, "Learn More" link to service detail page.
- **Design:** 3-column desktop grid, 2-column tablet, 1-column mobile.

**5. Why AJ / Proof-of-Pumpage Section**
- **Rationale (Peak moment — Peak-End Rule + thesis made visual):** This is the designed peak of the page — the single section that no competitor can replicate and that directly answers the core customer fear. Per strategic-validation.md §SF-06: "The gallon meter is the answer to every septic customer's deepest anxiety — it should be the hero-level proof point around which the entire brand narrative is built." This section makes the thesis visible and tangible.
- **Content:** Section headline referencing the gallon meter ("We Measure Every Drop — You Pay Only for What We Remove"), 3–4 differentiator columns or cards:
  1. Proof-of-Pumpage: Electronic solid-state gallon meters — documented, measured waste removal
  2. 40+ Years Combined Expertise: Founders James Ashman and Gerry Jones, combined experience claim
  3. Hill Country Roots: Local family-owned, based in Dripping Springs, not commuting from Austin
  4. TCEQ-Approved Disposal: Waste hauled to approved facilities only
- **Visual:** Photo of the electronic gallon meter in use (or a close-up of the meter readout). This is the photography priority — the meter is the moat.
- **Trust categories:** Competence + Risk Reduction + Character (all three covered in one section).

**6. Process — "What to Expect on Pumping Day"**
- **Rationale (Progressive Disclosure — anxiety reduction before conversion):** 0/5 competitors explain the service visit. Process transparency directly reduces the "what are they doing to my property?" anxiety that prevents first-time customers from booking. functionality-ideas.md Priority 4 identifies this as the primary surface for the gallon-meter differentiator narrative. Per WI-trust-psychology.md (via trust-analysis.md §FIRST-MOVER): this is a Character signal that no competitor provides.
- **Content:** 5-step visual process (per functionality-ideas.md Idea 14):
  1. We arrive and locate your tank access
  2. We record your tank's volume with our electronic gallon meter
  3. We pump and inspect visible components
  4. We walk you through our findings on-site
  5. Waste goes to a TCEQ-approved facility — every load documented
- **Format:** Horizontal step-indicator on desktop (numbered steps with icons), vertical on mobile. Each step: number, short title, 1-sentence description.
- **Note:** Step 2 names the gallon meter explicitly — second appearance of the differentiator in the page flow (first in hero/Why AJ, here in process context).

**7. Pumping Frequency Estimator (Interactive Tool)**
- **Rationale (Engagement + conversion trigger):** functionality-ideas.md Priority 1. Creates a "I need to act now" moment when results show an overdue tank. Positions AJ as educator — consistent with strategic-validation.md §7 tone directive ("educational and transparent, not salesy"). 0/5 competitors offer this tool. Drives repeat visits when users return annually to re-check.
- **Content:** Two-input form (household size + tank size in gallons), output = recommended pumping interval in years + "Your tank is overdue — schedule now" CTA when result < 1 year. Can incorporate tank size lookup by home age (functionality-ideas.md Idea 6) as a "don't know your size?" secondary input.
- **Section heading:** "Is Your Tank Overdue?" or "Septic Health Check"
- **Placement rationale:** After Process (position 6) because users who just learned how AJ works are primed to check if they personally need service. This sequencing converts information-seekers into leads.
- **Implementation:** Vanilla JS, two `<select>` elements, one `<p>` output — fully buildable per functionality-ideas.md §5.

**8. Service Area**
- **Rationale (Progressive Disclosure — "Are you near me?"):** 4/5 competitors display service areas. AJ currently has service cities as prose text only — no linked city pages, no map, no visual grid. Per strategic-validation.md §7: "City landing pages are the highest-ROI SEO move available." The homepage service area section is the entry point to that SEO infrastructure.
- **Content:**
  - Static SVG service area map (Hays, Travis, Comal counties highlighted) — functionality-ideas.md Idea 10
  - City grid below map: Dripping Springs · Austin · Kyle · New Braunfels · Driftwood · Wimberley · and surrounding communities
  - Each city links to its individual city landing page
  - ZIP code widget ("Do we serve your area?") — functionality-ideas.md Idea 2
- **Design:** Map + city grid layout. Map on left (desktop), city grid on right. Full-width on mobile.

---

**9. Testimonials**
- **Rationale (Social proof mid-page — emotional trust):** 4/5 competitors display named testimonials. AJ has zero on-site. Per trust-analysis.md §MUST: "AJ has zero on-site testimonials; every visit is a cold start." The mid-page testimonials position is per design-research.md §4: "Testimonials mid-page = emotional trust" (second trust deployment in the progressive trust distribution pattern).
- **Content:** 3–6 named testimonials pulled from Google reviews. Each card: star rating, review text (shortened), first name + last initial, review date (recency signal — no competitor shows dates, making this a differentiator). Link to full Reviews page.
- **Placeholder rule:** Per HANDOFF.md §2-2: "Never fabricate testimonials. Use `items: []` with a comment if none available." If real testimonials are not available at launch, omit this section entirely and add it when real reviews are gathered. Do not use placeholder copy.
- **Design:** 3-column desktop, single-column mobile. Card layout with star rating top-left.

**10. FAQ Teaser**
- **Rationale (Anxiety reduction before final CTA + FAQPage schema):** 2/5 competitors use FAQ sections on the homepage. AJ already has a FAQ page — the teaser drives to it and earns FAQPage JSON-LD schema (zero competitors have schema of any kind per strategic-validation.md §SF-02). Positioned just before the CTA banner to catch the "I have one more question" visitor before they leave.
- **Content:** Top 4 Q&A pairs (short answers — 1–2 sentences each):
  1. How often does a septic tank need to be pumped?
  2. What is proof-of-pumpage and why does it matter?
  3. Do you serve [primary city]?
  4. What happens if I skip pumping too long?
- "See all FAQs" link to full FAQ page.
- **Schema:** FAQPage JSON-LD wrapping the 4 visible Q&A pairs. First-mover advantage — no competitor has FAQ schema.

**11. CTA Banner**
- **Rationale (Recency effect — designed end-action):** The final content section before the footer captures visitors who scrolled the entire page and are now ready to act. Two CTA paths must be present: the phone-first emergency path and the form-first scheduled-service path. Per design-research.md §6: "Two-CTA convention is standard: one primary action button and one secondary softer action."
- **Content:**
  - Section headline: action-oriented, urgency framing ("Ready to Schedule?" or "Don't Wait for a Backup")
  - Primary CTA button: "Get a Free Quote" (links to multi-step form)
  - Secondary CTA: "Call (512) 956-0047" (tel: link) — for emergency callers
  - Supporting line: "24/7 Emergency Service Available · Serving Dripping Springs, Austin & Hill Country"
- **Design:** Dark-background section (navy or charcoal — distinct from footer). High-contrast CTA buttons. Section should feel like a visual anchor, not a soft afterthought.

  - Trust footer: "TCEQ Licensed · Insured · Serving Central Texas Since 2018"
  - Copyright + Privacy Policy link
- **Design:** Multi-column desktop (logo/phone | nav | cities | trust), single-column mobile.

## 7. Composition Score Target Justification (Stage 4 Input)

The 12-section sequence is structured to achieve the ≥80 Composition Score target (WI-design-psychology.md §3) by satisfying the following design-psychology principles:

- **Primacy captured:** Hero opens with the brand moat (proof-of-pumpage) — highest-value information first.
- **Early trust lock-in:** Trust Strip at position 2 ensures credentials appear before any scrolling past the hero.
- **Progressive disclosure arc:** Information reveals in the exact order a visitor needs it (who you are → why trust you → what you offer → how it works → is my tank due → are you near me → what do others say → any questions → act now).
- **Peak moment designed:** Why AJ / Proof-of-Pumpage at position 5 is the emotional and rational peak — the section a visitor would cite if asked "what was most memorable about that site?"
- **Recency captured:** CTA Banner as second-to-last section — a visitor who reaches the end of the page is ready to convert, and the final action prompt captures that readiness.
- **Trust distributed in 3 bands:** Trust Strip (position 2) → Testimonials (position 9) → Footer credentials (position 12). This mirrors the Parker & Sons / Genz-Ryan pattern documented in design-research.md §4.
- **Cognitive load managed:** Audience pathways (position 3) resolve the segmentation question before the services grid (position 4) asks visitors to evaluate individual offerings. Without this ordering, a homeowner and a restaurant manager both land on the same services grid without context.
- **Interactive engagement point:** The Pumping Frequency Estimator (position 7) breaks the passive scroll pattern with an active decision tool, extending time-on-page and creating a concrete conversion moment.
